Biography

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Baylor University. Prior to joining Baylor, I was a visiting scholar hosted by Jianxi Gao in the CS department at RPI. I obtained a PhD in Applied Math from Department of Mathematical Sciences, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in August 2023, working under the supervision of Kristin Bennett. I have published many paper in top ML conferences, including NeurIPS, AAAI, ICML, CLeaR etc. I have served as a PC member for several international conferences, such as NeurIPS, AAAI, ICML, ICLR, AISTATS etc.

Research

I am generally interested in machine/deep learning, optimization, and trustworthy AI, with a primary focus on
  • Neural Marked Temporal Point Process & Event Sequences;
  • Causal Inference & Learning;
  • (Deep) Generative Models;
  • Neural Network Training and Dynamics;
  • Machine Learning for Health.
